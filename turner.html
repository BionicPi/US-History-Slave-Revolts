Nat turner was born a slave in Virginia, and was sold multiple times in his life. He was educated in religion, reading, and writing, and found examples in the bible opposing slavery. 
He believed he was sent to free the slaves.

An August 1831 eclipse was taken as an omen, and 60 slaves rose, killing at least 55 white men, women, and children. The militia dispersed the rebellion, killing 40 and placing 15 heads on posts as a warning to others who might consider rebellion.

The rebellion caused the Virginia Assembly to consider provisions for gradual emancipation, but the bill failed, and the south instead tightened restrictions.
